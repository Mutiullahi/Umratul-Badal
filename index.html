Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Umrah Badal (Proxy Umrah)? +
Umrah Badal is when a Muslim performs Umrah on behalf of another person who is unable to do so — due to illness, old age, disability, or death. It is supported by authentic Islamic scholarship.
Is Proxy Umrah permitted in Islam? +
Yes. The Prophet ﷺ permitted Hajj to be performed on behalf of those who are unable. The majority of Islamic scholars consider Umrah Badal similarly permissible.
Can I order it for a deceased family member? +
Yes. You may order an Umrah Badal or Hajj Badal for a deceased person as an act of Sadaqah Jariyah. Please provide their full name when booking.
What proof will I receive? +
Within 72 hours: an HD video, photos from the Holy Mosque, an official digital certificate with the person's name, and written confirmation from our scholar.
Who performs the Umrah or Hajj? +
All rituals are performed by qualified Islamic scholars (Ulama) residing in or near Makkah. They perform every ritual with sincerity and according to Shariah.
Is this service certified and trustworthy? +
Yes. Umratul Badal is a legally registered, fully accountable organisation. We operate with complete transparency and have served clients across 40+ countries.
