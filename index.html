Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Umrah Badal (Proxy Umrah)?
+
Umrah Badal is when a Muslim performs Umrah on behalf of another person who is unable to do so themselves — due to illness, old age, disability, or death. It is an act of worship and mercy supported by Islamic scholarship.
Is Proxy Umrah permitted in Islam?
+
Yes. Performing Umrah on behalf of another is supported by authentic Hadith. The Prophet ﷺ permitted Hajj to be performed on behalf of those who are unable. The majority of Islamic scholars consider Umrah Badal similarly permissible.
Can I order it for a deceased family member?
+
Yes. You may order an Umrah Badal or Hajj Badal for a deceased person as an act of Sadaqah Jariyah (ongoing charity). Please provide their full name when booking.
What proof will I receive?
+
Within 72 hours of the service being performed, you will receive: an HD video of the rituals, photos from the Holy Mosque, an official digital certificate with the person's name, and a written confirmation from our scholar.
Who performs the Umrah or Hajj?
+
All rituals are performed by qualified Islamic scholars (Ulama) and their trusted staff who reside in or near Makkah. They have deep religious knowledge and perform every ritual with sincerity and according to Shariah.
Is this service legally registered and trustworthy?
+
Yes. Umratul Badal is a legally registered, fully accountable organisation. Our registration documents are available for review on our Verification page. We operate with complete transparency and have served clients across 40+ countries.
